INTRODUCTION
Historic, cultured, and buzzing with new developments, Tainan offers a tantalizing blend of attractions and activities for business travelers. The city—Taiwan's oldest and former capital—is easily accessible via high-speed rail, plus numerous upscale hotels have opened in the wake of urban rejuvenation.

Green Energy Base
Green Energy Base
Tainan's green energy industries are transforming, with solar energy, LED lighting, and smart electric vehicles fast becoming the foundation of the green energy industry ecosystem. The city government's intent is to have the Shalun Green Energy Science City embrace international standards to become a green energy policy and R&D center. Adjacent to International Convention Center Tainan, the Science City is an ideal venue for sustainable activities.

Art of Temples
Tainan Confucius Temple
There is no set procedure to visiting a temple, and no tickets are ever required. If one happens to see a temple while walking in Tainan, as long as you have appreciation and respect, you are welcome to go in and take a look. In order to dedicate the best to the gods, almost every part of a temple is filled with gorgeous craftsmanship.

Culture and Legacies
Wu Family Garden
Anping Fort, built by the Dutch in 1634, and Anping Old Street, where local street food and souvenirs abound, are popular historical sites. Anping Tree House, a former warehouse engulfed by banyan trees, often serves as an event venue. The Jingzaijiao Tile-Paved Salt Field is the oldest salt field in Taiwan, where visitors can experience traditional salt harvesting and drying, followed by a visit to the Taiwan Salt Museum to learn about the history of salt production in Taiwan and around the world.

Magnificent Mangroves
Sicao Green Tunnel
Take a bamboo raft ride through the wetlands of Taijiang National Park and learn about its ecosystem that supports numerous fish, mammal and bird species, including the endangered blackfaced spoonbill. Particularly enchanting is the Sicao Green Tunnel, where trees stretch overhead and are reflected in the still waters.
