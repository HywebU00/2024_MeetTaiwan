Industrial Clusters
Taichung
Taichung is home to some of Taiwan's—and the world's—major manufacturers: Giant Bicycles, chipmaker TSMC, Garmin, and hundreds of multinational companies conduct research and production in Taichung’s industrial zones. There are also regular exhibitions to showcase the local industries.

Cultural Heritage
Taichung government office
Taichung not only has the appearance of a modern international city, it also manages to retain unique ancient cultural sites such as the Wufeng Lin Family Mansion and Garden, Dajia Jenn Lann Temple, Taichung Prefectural Hall, and many others.

Protected Wetlands
Charlotte Park
Home to over 120 species of birds, the Gaomei Wetlands on the western coast of Taichung is a scenic spot for bird watching, learning about intertidal habitats, or simply enjoying the sunset—with rows of wind turbines forming iconic silhouettes along the shore.
Gaomei Wetland

Bike Route
Taichung New City Hall
Explore the northern fringe of Taichung on two wheels with a tour along the 4.5 km HouFeng Bikeway, which was converted from a railroad track. This easy ride features a section of old railway tunnel as well as a crossing over a steel bridge, where cyclists are treated to sweeping views of the Dajia River valley.

Theater for Arts
National Taichung Theater
For groups into architecture, construction, or creative industries, the National Taichung Theater is a masterpiece to be studied and is very suitable for performance events. Designed by Pritzker Prize-winning architect Toyo Ito, the building is famed for its specially molded curved walls and organically flowing interiors.
